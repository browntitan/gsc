T&C 12 - Supply Chain Terms

Clause 4 - Confidentiality
For T&C 12 Clause 4, confidentiality obligations should meet the minimum company standard for protected information, permitted disclosures, and survival period. Any survival shorter than two years requires approval.

Confidentiality must meet the company minimum standard. Shortened survival periods need approval.

Clause 5 - Indemnification
For T&C 12 Clause 5, indemnification carve-outs must remain within the approved template. Any expansion to indirect, consequential, third-party regulatory, or open-ended affiliate claims requires legal review and deviation approval.

Keep indemnification carve-outs within the approved template. Escalate broadened carve-outs to legal and deviation review.

If the supplier asks for reciprocal indemnity language under T&C 12 Clause 5, the reviewer should compare it to the standard fallback. Reciprocal language that shifts product, IP, or regulatory exposure back to the company must be redlined or escalated.

Do not accept reciprocal indemnity that moves product, IP, or regulatory risk to the company without escalation.

T&C 19 - Commercial Terms

Clause 7 - Termination
For T&C 19 Clause 7, termination language should preserve termination for cause, define a reasonable cure period, and avoid supplier-only convenience termination unless specifically approved by procurement leadership.

Preserve termination for cause, ensure cure rights, and avoid one-sided supplier convenience termination.

When reviewing T&C 19 Clause 7, notice and cure periods should be commercially reasonable. Cure periods longer than thirty days for operational breach should be escalated if they delay mitigation or replacement planning.

Escalate cure periods above thirty days where they create operational exposure.

Clause 8 - Limitation of Liability
For T&C 19 Clause 8, liability caps should align to the approved cap framework. Reviewers should look for uncapped claims, super caps, and carve-outs that effectively nullify the negotiated cap.

Compare the clause to the approved liability-cap framework and escalate super caps or overly broad carve-outs.

In T&C 19 Clause 8, indirect and consequential damages should normally remain excluded on a mutual basis. Supplier edits that restore these damages for broad categories of claims should be treated as a material deviation.

Mutual indirect-damages exclusions are preferred. Broad restoration of indirect damages is a material deviation.
